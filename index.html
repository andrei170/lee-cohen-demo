What you already have

The infrastructure is mostly built. The acquisition layer isn't.

You already operate the back end of an investor funnel: an accreditation form on the RedHoek+ site, a software flow that verifies and routes investor packages, a quarterly distribution cadence, and a real estate operation that has earned the right to sell investors capital appreciation.

What is missing is the front end: the system that gets 50, 100, 200 qualified investor conversations into that funnel every quarter without you or Karla making the calls personally.

  • Existing: investor backend, accreditation flow, quarterly investor mailings to current LPs
  • Missing: outbound investor sequencing, authority layer, lead capture for warm inbound
  • Reference operators: Ryan Pineda, Grant Cardone, raising 9-figures via the systems you do not yet have
One missing layer · three symptoms

Same root cause, three faces of it.

Each of the three quotes below came from our call. They sound like separate problems. They are not. They are the same missing front-end of the investor funnel, showing up three different ways.

01

Investor acquisition is 100% manual outbound, with zero pipeline.

"For investors, we don't have any leads at this point, zero. It's more outbound, it's more us doing research on who's investing in real estate and then trying to call them."
Lee, 12:04 →

The cost: every raise restarts from zero. The ten people Karla called this quarter are not the ten she calls next quarter. There is no compounding pipeline.

The fix: a sequenced outbound system targeting PE firms by alternative-asset filter, family offices by AUM, and accredited individuals by liquidity event. Owned by you, run by software, not dependent on personal network depth.

02

Brand recognition is already happening offline, but not converting online.

"I'll go to a meeting and someone will be like, oh, I recently saw that video. Or I'll be in a convention and be like, oh, we saw your project that you're building."
Lee, 8:02 →

The cost: the recognition is real but ephemeral. People remember the project, then have nowhere to follow up. No lead capture on RedHoek+ for them. No retargeting layer.

The fix: the same authority footprint that makes people say "I saw your project", sequenced into a capture system. Specifically: an investor-only opt-in on RedHoek+, a quarterly investor briefing PDF as the lead magnet, retargeting from social into the briefing.

03

The marketing line item exists in every quarterly meeting but never gets built.

"Every quarter at our company meeting it's always on there that we want to do marketing... we're contemplating hiring an in-house marketer just to basically run the point."
Lee, 10:46 →

The cost: a full-time marketing hire at the level RedHoek+ needs is six figures plus equity, and most of them have never raised PE capital. Wrong skill set, wrong cost structure, slow time-to-value.

The fix: contracted partner who builds the system in less than 7 days, runs it for the first quarter, and either hands it off to the in-house hire later or stays on as the outsourced operator. Faster than recruiting, smaller cap-ex, easier to course-correct.

The build

Two pieces. Built in less than 7 days.

The front-end is two assets and one cadence. Not a stack of deliverables. Not a generic content plan. Just the targeted sequence that fills your existing back end, plus the asset that makes the sequence work. Owned by RedHoek+ when we are done.

Piece 1 · The Sequence

Targeted investor email sequence that uses Crexie-grade data, not Facebook.

A 4–5 step email sequence written in the language of family offices and PE allocators, sent from a deliverability-warmed RedHoek+ inbox. The make-or-break is the targeting layer: not consumer-grade scrapers, but the same niche real-estate-investor data sources Lauren mentioned on the call (CoStar / Crexie style, plus PE filings and family-office databases). That is where most marketers fail this brief.

  • Targeting: PE shops by alternative-real-estate mandate, family offices by AUM, individuals by liquidity event
  • Sequence: intro + portfolio + Solina case + investor briefing + final ask
  • Routing: replies land in Karla's inbox with pre-qualification context already attached
  • Screener-grade footprint: light LinkedIn + portfolio surface so the email does not get spam-filtered mentally by the analyst who Googles RedHoek+ before forwarding to the partner
  • Tracking: open + reply rates by segment, monthly reporting
Piece 2 · The Asset

Quarterly investor briefing PDF as the asset that makes piece 1 work.

A 10–14 page RedHoek+ investor briefing, current portfolio, active raises, market outlook, returns history, designed in your brand. Doubles as the email payload (gives the recipient something concrete to forward up the chain) and the warm-inbound capture on the RedHoek+ site (gated by an investor-accreditation opt-in that routes into the back end you already operate).

  • Format: 10–14 page designed PDF, refreshed each quarter
  • Capture opt-in: name, email, accredited yes/no, target check size
  • Routes to: the investor backend you already have, no new system
  • Email payload: attached or linked from the cold sequence so the analyst has something to send up the chain
The 7-day install

From greenlight to live in under one week.

Day 1–3
